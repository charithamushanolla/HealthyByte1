Pushups


1. Start in a plank position. Your core should be tight, shoulders pulled down and back, and your neck neutral.

2. Bend your elbows and begin to lower your body down to the floor. When your chest grazes it, extend your elbows and return to the start.

3. If you can’t quite perform a standard pushup with good form, drop down to a modified stance on your knees.

4. Complete 3 sets of as many reps as possible.


Side Lunge


1. Start standing with legs.

2. slightly wider than shoulder-distance apart and toes pointed forward.

3. Shift your body weight to one leg bending the knee until it reaches a 90-degree angle the other leg is straight.

4. Glutes are pressing back behind you. Return to center and switch sides.


Standing overhead dumbbell presses


1. Pick a light set of dumbbell start by standing, either with your feet shoulder-width apart or staggered.

2. Move the weights overhead so your upper arms are parallel to the floor.

3. Bracing your core, begin to push up until your arms are fully extended above your head.

4. After a brief pause, bend your elbows and lower the weight back down until your tricep is parallel to the floor again.

5. Complete 3 sets of 12 reps.


Single-leg deadlifts


1. Begin standing with a dumbbell in your right hand and your knees slightly bent.

2. Hinging at the hips, begin to kick your left leg straight back behind you, lowering the dumbbell down toward the ground.

3. When you reach a comfortable height with your left leg, slowly return to the starting position in a controlled motion.

4. Repeat 10 to 12 reps before moving the weight to your left hand and repeating the same steps on the left leg.


Side planks


1. Lie on your right side with your left leg and foot stacked on top of your right leg and foot.

2. Prop your upper body up by placing your right forearm on the ground, elbow directly under your shoulder.

3. Contract your core to stiffen your spine and lift your hips and knees off the ground, forming a straight line with your body.

4. Return to start in a controlled manner. Repeat 3 sets of 10-15 reps on one side, then switch.


Situps


1. Start by lying on the ground on your back with your knees bent, feet flat, and your hands behind your head.

2. Keeping your feet glued to the ground, begin to roll up from your head, engaging your core throughout.

3. When your chest reaches your legs, begin the controlled phase back down to the starting position.

4. Complete 3 sets of 15 reps as a beginner.


Squats


1. Start by standing straight, with your feet slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, and your arms at your sides.

2. Keeping your chest and chin up, push your hips back and bend your knees as if you’re going to sit in a chair.

3. Ensuring your knees don’t bow inward or outward, drop down until your thighs are parallel to the ground.

4. Complete 3 sets of 20 reps.


Lunges


1. Stand tall with feet hip-width apart. Engage your core.

2. Take a big step forward with right leg. Start to shift your weight forward so heel hits the floor first.

3. Lower your body until right thigh is parallel to the floor and right shin is vertical.

4. It’s OK if knee shifts forward a little as long as it doesn’t go past right toe.

5. Press into right heel to drive back up to starting position.


Jumping Jack


1. Stand upright with your legs together, arms at your sides.

2. Bend your knees slightly, and jump into the air.

3. As you jump, spread your legs to be about shoulder-width apart. Stretch your arms out and over your head.

4. Jump back to starting position.

5. Repeat.


Hip Bridges


1. Lie face up on the floor, with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.

2. Keep your arms at your side with your palms down.

3. Lift your hips off the ground until your knees, hips and shoulders form a straight line.

4. Squeeze those glutes hard and keep your abs drawn in so you don’t overextend your back during the exercise.

5. Hold your bridged position for a couple of seconds before easing back down.
